# Where to Watch the 2026 FIFA World Cup Across Massachusetts: The Complete Boston-Area Guide

The single most important answer: Massachusetts offers hundreds of ways to watch the 2026 World Cup, anchored by the free **FIFA Fan Festival at Boston City Hall Plaza** (June 12–27) and the seven matches at Gillette Stadium ("Boston Stadium") in Foxborough — but the marquee Fan Festival is capped at 5,000 people per session and already massively oversubscribed, so most fans will land at one of the city's soccer bars, brewery screenings, or dozens of free municipal watch parties.

**TL;DR**
- The free **FIFA Fan Festival at City Hall Plaza** is the central hub (June 12–27, group stage only), but it's capped at 5,000/session with advance registration required and entry NOT guaranteed — nearly 150,000 people registered against that cap and the opening weekend sold out (Meet Boston CEO Martha Sheridan, via WBUR, June 12: "More than 5,000 people filled out registrations and wanted to be entered into the lottery to get entrance").
- Regional activity peaks on Gillette Stadium's **seven match days — June 13, 16, 19, 23, 26 (group stage), June 29 (Round of 32), and July 9 (Quarterfinal)** — when Patriot Place's 20+ bars/restaurants, Cambridge's seven outdoor parties, and most soccer-bar crowds are at their busiest.
- Beyond downtown, Massachusetts awarded **$10 million to 17 recipients across 25 communities** (Sports and Entertainment Events Fund Grant Program, Healey-Driscoll Administration) funding free family-friendly watch parties in Cambridge, Revere, Chelsea, Quincy, Worcester, Brockton, Lexington, Everett, Lowell, Marlborough, Franklin and more — while established soccer bars host specific national-team and club fan communities.

## Key Findings

**Gillette Stadium ("Boston Stadium") hosts seven matches** — the gravitational center of regional activity:
- **Sat June 13, 9:00 p.m.** — Haiti vs. Scotland (Group C)
- **Tue June 16, 6:00 p.m.** — Iraq vs. Norway (Group I)
- **Fri June 19, 6:00 p.m.** — Scotland vs. Morocco (Group C)
- **Tue June 23, 4:00 p.m.** — England vs. Ghana (Group L)
- **Fri June 26, 3:00 p.m.** — Norway vs. France (Group I)
- **Mon June 29, 4:30 p.m.** — Round of 32
- **Thu July 9, 4:00 p.m.** — Quarterfinal

Eight nations are scheduled in Foxborough: France, England, Norway, Scotland, Haiti, Iraq, Morocco, and Ghana. The highest-ranked are third-seed France (FIFA ranking 3) and fourth-seed England (ranking 4), with Morocco ranked 7 (per CBS Boston/NESN). Gillette is at 1 Patriot Place, about 29 miles south of downtown Boston ("about 29 miles south of Boston," per CBS Boston); the MBTA runs special-event commuter rail to Foxboro Station ($80 roundtrip, roughly 4x normal fare) for match-ticket holders only. Non-ticket-holders are actively discouraged from traveling to Foxborough and encouraged to watch in Boston. The tournament's regional footprint is large: Massachusetts officials project over 2 million visitors and roughly $1.1 billion in economic impact to New England (Jeevan Ramapriya, MA Office of International Trade and Investment, via WBUR/NBC Boston: "We should expect over 2 million visitors… a billion dollars in economic output").

The single biggest concentration of casual viewing happens on those seven match days. The listings below are organized by category.

## Details

### 1. The Official FIFA Fan Festival (the central hub)

**FIFA Fan Festival™ Boston — City Hall Plaza**, 1 City Hall Square, Boston (Government Center/State T stops).
- **Dates:** Daily June 12–27 (16 days, group stage only — it does NOT run during the knockout rounds).
- **Hours:** Vary by day, generally early afternoon to late evening (e.g., June 12: 2 p.m.–11:30 p.m.; June 15: 11 a.m.–8:30 p.m.; June 27: 3 p.m.–12:30 a.m.).
- **Format:** 2–3 matches per day on giant screens (all matches across the US/Mexico/Canada, not just Foxborough games), plus a Cultural Showcase with local music, dance, and performers.
- **Food/drink:** Local vendors including RedRed Kitchen, Dumpling Daughter, Stillwater Grille, The Sausage Guy, Si Cara Pizza, Summer Shack, Che Empanada, and El Jeffe's.
- **Admission:** FREE but advance registration required; capacity capped at 5,000 per session. A pass does NOT guarantee entry — for high-demand matches (e.g., USA games, Haiti-Scotland), a lottery is used and overflow is directed to other city sites. As of opening weekend, the first Friday and Saturday sold out and nearly 150,000 registrations had been received.
- **Vibe:** The marquee city-center atmosphere with the biggest crowds and screens.

### 2. Foxborough / Gillette Stadium area on match days — Patriot Place "Summer of Soccer"

**Patriot Place** (adjacent to the stadium) runs a "Summer of Soccer" program with 20+ bars and restaurants. On the seven match days, most go first-come, first-served with no reservations:
- **Scorpion Bar** — Mexican cuisine/cocktails, big screen, capacity up to 420, no cover; extended match-day hours (June 13/16/19: 12 p.m.–12:45 a.m.; June 23/26/29 & July 9: 10 a.m.–12:45 a.m.).
- **The Harp** — comfort food, 35-foot big-screen TV, best stadium views; no reservations on match days.
- **Six String Grill & Stage** — showing every match except midnight kickoffs; 90-minute table limit on match days.
- **Bar Louie** — 22 TVs incl. rooftop bar; first-come, first-served; daily 11 a.m.–1 a.m.
- **Citizen Crust** — globally inspired food, every match on screens, beer buckets.
- **Splitsville | Howl at the Moon | Golf** — all-inclusive pregame parties (ticketed).
- **Davio's** — "Macallan Lounge" elevated experience (ticketed, June 13–29).
- Plus Estella, Tavolino, Olive Garden, Red Robin, Saga Hibachi, Twenty8, and quick-bite spots.
- Between matches: Crazy Axes, ELEVENO pickleball, Muse Paintbar, live music on the Dean College Stage, Laugh Patriot Place comedy.
- **Vibe:** Family-friendly by day, energetic match-day crowds; Uber/MBTA encouraged. Note Patriot Place sits in a small town (~18,000), so the bigger non-ticket-holder atmosphere is in Boston itself.

## Recommendations

**For the biggest atmosphere without a stadium ticket:** Target the FIFA Fan Festival at City Hall Plaza, but register IMMEDIATELY for your desired day — it's capped at 5,000 and routinely oversubscribed, so have a backup since a pass doesn't guarantee entry. If shut out, the nearby Dubliner, High Street Place, and The Greatest Bar are walkable alternatives.

**On the seven Gillette match days (June 13, 16, 19, 23, 26, 29; July 9):** Expect peak crowds everywhere. Arrive 30+ minutes early for group-stage games and 60+ minutes for knockouts. If you have a match ticket, take the MBTA special commuter rail to Foxboro Station and pregame at Patriot Place. If not, stay in Boston/Cambridge — non-ticket-holders are discouraged from traveling to Foxborough.

**To watch with a specific country's or club's fans:** Scotland → The Haven (JP) or The Greatest Bar; USA → The Banshee; France → Warehouse or Rochambeau; Brazil → Fogo de Chão; England → Phoenix Landing; Arsenal/Real Madrid/Spurs/Man United → Dillon's / Elephant & Castle / LongCross / Dubliner respectively. For Latino/Spanish-language viewing, head to Chelsea's Fiesta de Fútbol.

**For families/all ages:** The free municipal parties (Cambridge United, Revere Field of Play, Worcester Common, Lexington, Everett) are the best bets — face painting, soccer activities, food trucks, and no alcohol requirement. CanalSide (Cambridge) and Patriot Place by day are also family-friendly.

**Decision thresholds that should change your plan:**
- *If the USA advances deep into the knockouts,* watch for additional pop-up parties (Brockton flagged a possible U.S. knockout-round party; the City of Boston runs semifinal/final parties July 14, 15, 19).
- *If your target is the July 9 Quarterfinal or July 19 Final,* note the Fan Fest closes June 27 — pivot to Lawn on D, SOJUba (Fenway final party), Revere's Final Festival, Worcester Common, or Bow Market for those late-tournament dates.
- *If you can't secure a Fan Fest pass within 48 hours of your match,* default immediately to a soccer bar matched to your team (arrive early) rather than risking being turned away at City Hall Plaza.

## Caveats

- **This is a fast-moving, late-organized event.** Many logistics (watch-party licenses, bar 3 a.m. closing approvals, train pricing) were finalized only weeks before kickoff; details, dates, and times can change. Confirm directly with venues before traveling.
- **Knockout-stage matchups (Round of 32 on June 29, Quarterfinal July 9) were not yet determined** while the group stage was ongoing — opponents at Gillette and at watch parties for those dates were TBD at publication.
- **Fan Festival entry is not guaranteed even with registration.** The 5,000-person cap and lottery system mean many registrants will be turned away for popular matches.
- **Crowd-size estimates vary by source** — e.g., Scotland traveling-fan figures range from ~10,000 (Association of Tartan Army Clubs) to MBTA/press projections of 20,000–30,000. Treat all attendance numbers as estimates.
- **Some venue capacities, exact hours, and reservation policies vary;** ticketed events (Davio's Macallan Lounge, Splitsville pregame, Lucky Strike lanes) require separate booking.
- **Coverage of certain fan communities (Portugal, Mexico) is thinner in published guides;** the options listed for them are best-available rather than confirmed dedicated supporter HQs.
